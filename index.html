Your Query:

My landlord is refusing to return my security deposit after I moved out, even though I left the apartment in good condition. What are my rights as a tenant in South Africa?

General Legal Information:

In South Africa, tenant rights regarding security deposits are primarily governed by the Rental Housing Act and the Consumer Protection Act. Here are some key points:

Security Deposit Regulations:

  • Landlords can request a security deposit, typically not exceeding one or two months' rent.
  • The deposit must be held in an interest-bearing account for the duration of the lease.
  • Upon termination of the lease, the landlord must return the deposit with interest, minus any legitimate deductions.

Valid Deductions:

  • Unpaid rent or utilities
  • Costs for repairing damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Cleaning costs if the property was not returned in a reasonably clean condition

Your Rights:

  • You have the right to receive an itemized list of deductions if any are made.
  • The landlord must provide proof of costs for any repairs claimed.
  • If no deductions are made, the deposit must be returned within 7-14 days after the lease ends.
  • You can dispute unreasonable deductions through the Rental Housing Tribunal.

Note: This is general information. The specific details of your rental agreement and provincial regulations may affect your situation. Consult with a qualified attorney for advice tailored to your circumstances.
